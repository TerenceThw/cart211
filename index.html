Info

If you'd like to explore my work, feel free to visit my website. I designed it to let users fully express their creativity, encouraging them to create using simple lines and shapes.

Here's a little bit about me:

I'm currently a student pursuing a bachelor's degree in Computational Art. My passion for traditional oil painting began when I was a kid. Not only did I enjoy creating them, but I also found joy in observing them. Every time I visit a museum and look at these masterpieces, I marvel at how they've endured over time and still captivate viewers. Despite the years that have passed, these works continue to connect with audiences. This has always made me wonder: How can I create something aesthetically pleasing while telling a story that represents me—without worrying about whether viewers will like it or not.

As I've grown, I've discovered immersive art—an art form that creates a multisensory environment, inviting visitors to explore and interact with the space. Unlike traditional art, which you simply observe, immersive installations engage multiple senses, allowing for a deeper experience. The media used to create these works also reflect the era we live in—the evolution of technology. Just as tempera paints represented the Middle Ages and Renaissance, or oil pigments marked later periods, I believe that technology is the medium that defines our time. It has the power to transform the impossible into the possible, providing endless space for artistic exploration.

As an artist, I love experimenting with different media, especially blending traditional elements with new technologies. My work often interacts with space, light, sound, and other traditional media. I'm still in the process of discovering new ways to create immersive experiences.
