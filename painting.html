This is a still life drawing I created using HeavyPaint. In this work, I focused on shapes and colors instead of lines. By doing so, I paid more attention to details I normally wouldn't notice.

This piece was made using HeavyPaint and came about spontaneously while I was eating a Happy Meal. There's no deeper meaning behind itâ€”it's just a simple, straightforward snapshot of an everyday moment, focusing on the shapes and colors I observed.

This piece is based on a photo I found on Pinterest. I didn't start with any outlines but instead began by painting large shapes. Afterward, I refined the image by adding details with smaller shapes. Using this method forces me to observe the image in terms of big shapes rather than over-focusing on small details.

When I created this painting, I wanted to convey a sense of messiness and a free, expressive style. To achieve this, I used two different brushes: a flat brush for the solid colors and another brush with a strong pattern. The patterned brush was primarily used to add details and enhance the feeling of messiness.
